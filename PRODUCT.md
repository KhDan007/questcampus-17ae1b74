# Product

## Register

product

## Users

QuestCampus serves students applying to universities across countries, especially
international applicants who need help turning a scattered admissions process into
one coherent plan. Many users are scholarship-sensitive, uncertain about requirements,
and switching between school research, essays, documents, Common App, direct portals,
and payment decisions.

## Product Purpose

QuestCampus is an application workspace that helps a student decide where to apply,
understand what each target requires, prepare every answer/document/essay, and move
ready applications into the browser under human control. Success means the student can
open the product and immediately know the next best action, why it matters, what
evidence supports it, and whether a university is ready for the extension or still
needs work.

## Brand Personality

Expert, direct, and motivating. QuestCampus should feel like a serious admissions
operator that does the hard synthesis for the student, not a generic chatbot or a
decorative college-search toy. The tone should be concrete, evidence-based, and
calmly ambitious.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI coaching interfaces that bury everything inside chat history.
- Vague dashboards with disabled CTAs and unclear next steps.
- Marketing-first SaaS pages inside the authenticated workspace.
- Advice like "improve your essays" or "look for scholarships" without a profile,
  target, deadline, or evidence link.
- Portals that imply an application was submitted when the human has only reached
  review-ready state.
- Unknown-portal autofill attempts that silently use student data before readiness
  and coverage are proven.

## Design Principles

1. The cockpit leads. The first authenticated screen should show roadmap state,
   blockers, scholarship path, extension readiness, and the next action without
   requiring chat.
2. Evidence is visible. Recommendations and tips must expose the profile fact,
   requirement, scholarship, or computed score behind them.
3. Readiness is a contract. If a target is not ready, the UI shows exact missing
   items and remediation routes; if it is ready, web and extension use the same
   target identity.
4. Human control is explicit. The agent and extension can prepare and fill, but the
   student reviews, submits, pays, and confirms applied status.
5. Dense beats decorative. The workspace should be scannable, responsive, and calm
   while preserving the established QuestCampus visual identity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, focus states, keyboard access, and reduced motion.
Use plain-language recovery states for stale data, missing evidence, package expiry,
unsupported portals, and affordability blockers. Do not rely on color alone for target
readiness, scholarship priority, risk, or application status.
